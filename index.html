Rules & Guidelines

📋 Event Structure

1. Problem Statement Release

  • Official problem statements will be released on 16th March 2026
  • Teams must choose 1 problem and come prepared with ideas, research, or initial design.

2. Team Formation

  • Team size should be 4 members (fixed after registration)
  • Cross-department teams are not allowed.
  • One member must be nominated as Team Leader.

3. Screening & Rounds

Level 1 – Idea Screening (Presentation Round):
  • Teams will present their approach, technical stack, and solution plan (8–10 minutes).
  • Top teams will be shortlisted for Level 2.
Level 2 – Presentation / Prototyping / Coding Round (Final):
  • Shortlisted teams should complete their Presentation / develop prototype / Coding.
  • Final demo + Q&A to the judging panel will be on 9th-11th April 2026

4. Judging Criteria

  • Innovation & Creativity – 10%
  • Problem Understanding – 10%
  • Technical Implementation – 10%
  • Working Demo / Prototype – 50%
  • Presentation & Teamwork – 20%

🏆 Prizes

1st Prize₹10,000
2nd Prize₹8,000
3rd Prize₹5,000

⚖️ Rules & Regulations

  • All work must be original and developed only during the hackathon (except initial research and design).
  • Use of open-source libraries/APIs is allowed with proper credits.
  • The decision of judges will be final.
  • Teams must adhere to timeline, discipline, and fair play.
  • Plagiarism, copying other teams' code, or unethical behavior will result in disqualification.
  • Bring your own laptops, chargers, and necessary tools.
  • Internet will be provided (if applicable).
  • Participants must be present in the venue throughout the event (no proxy).

📦 Deliverables

Teams must submit at final evaluation:

  1. Prototype / Demo
  2. Source Code / GitHub Link
  3. PPT (Max 8 Slides) covering:
    • Problem
    • Proposed solution
    • Architecture / Tech stack
    • Implementation
    • Results
    • Future scope

🤝 Code of Conduct

  • Maintain professionalism, teamwork, and respect for all.
  • No offensive content in ideas or presentations.
  • Coordinators reserve the right to modify rules in special cases.
